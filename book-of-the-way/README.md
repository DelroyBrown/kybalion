# The Book of the Way

> A living collection of observations, verses and parables about being human.

## Purpose

This book is not written to tell people what to think.

It exists to present observations about life in a way that encourages reflection rather than instruction.

No author is named.
The ideas belong to whoever finds truth in them.

---

## Principles

Every verse must:

- Stand on its own.
- Be true before it is clever.
- Be memorable.
- Be simple.
- Invite thought rather than explain itself.
- Earn its place.

If a verse can be improved, it should be rewritten.

---

## How We Write

We do not begin with wisdom.

We begin with observation.

Observations become seeds.
Seeds become verses.
Verses become books.

---

## The Seeds

Not every observation belongs in the book.

Some remain seeds for months or years before becoming verses.

## Book I — Time

1. Every person enters the world with a measure of time, yet no one knows the measure they have been given.

2. Tomorrow is a hope, never a promise.

3. The wise do not count their days; they make their days worth counting.

4. Many wait for the right day, not knowing they are living it.

5. Many spend their lives preparing for tomorrow, forgetting that life is happening today.
